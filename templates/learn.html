Common Scam Patterns

  • Fake Prizes: Messages claiming you've won a lottery, prize, or inheritance you never entered
  • Account Verification: Requests to verify your bank account or personal information urgently
  • Too Good to Be True: Offers promising quick money, free expensive items, or unrealistic returns
  • Impersonation: Messages pretending to be from banks, government agencies, or trusted companies
  • Phishing Links: Messages with suspicious links asking you to click immediately

Spotting Red Flags

  • Urgency: Messages creating panic or demanding immediate action
  • Suspicious Language: Poor grammar, spelling mistakes, or unusual phrasing
  • Unknown Sender: Messages from numbers or emails you don't recognize
  • Request for Money: Any request to send money, gift cards, or payment details
  • Personal Information: Asking for passwords, PINs, OTPs, or account details
  • Threatening Tone: Messages threatening legal action, account closure, or penalties
ğŸ”’

Essential Safety Tips

  • Verify First: Always verify messages by contacting the company directly through official channels
  • Never Share Sensitive Info: Don't share passwords, OTPs, or bank details via message or call
  • Be Skeptical: If something seems too good to be true, it probably is
  • Don't Click Suspicious Links: Avoid clicking links from unknown sources
  • Report Scams: Report suspicious messages to authorities and your service provider
  • Use Two-Factor Authentication: Enable 2FA on all important accounts for added security
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a message feels wrong, it probably is. Delete and block the sender
